Experience

Data Analytics Intern - Miami Heat

In the summer of 2023, I interned with the Miami Heat in their analytics department. During my time in Miami, I collaborated with the Revenue Strategy and Analytics team to mitigate errors and inconsistencies in ticketing reports for the Miami Heat and 601 Analytics clients. I achieved this by reviewing SQL queries, documenting report logic, and conducting product mapping and classification. As a result of my efforts, I was able to improve data accuracy and ensure precise categorization and organization of ticketing data within the company's data warehouse.




The NBA HBCU Fellowship Program provides career development opportunities for students from Historically Black College and Universities (HBCUs). I was selected as one of 74 fellows out of over 5000 applicants to be a part of this program. During the program I was able to connect with other HBCU fellows through heartfelt conversations, educated on social justice concerns and servant leadership.




Graduate Research Assistant - NC A&T

During my graduate program at NC A&T I successfully defended my master's project on a web-based visualization dashboard for a recommendation tool. In order to accomplish this, I conducted in-depth research on emerging technologies and methodologies in the field of both recommendation engines and web-based dashboards. I created an interactive dashboard using Python's Dash framework as the front-end interface intended for a Tool for Recommending Attack Patterns (TRAP). The TRAP tool uses topic modeling to recommend relevant attack patterns from the Common Attack Pattern Enumeration and Classification (CAPEC) database. As a result, the dashboard educated users on attack patterns, their severity and likelihood. Additionally, it led to improved decision making for cybersecurity strategies. Furthermore, I co-authored a paper titled Visualization Dashboard for Recommending Attack Patterns Using Topic Modeling that was accepted into the 12th International Conference on Software and Information Engineering (ICSIE 2023).
You can view the research paper here.

Check out the prototype of my dashboard along with other projects in the project section in my website. It is labeled CAPEC Interactive Dashboard.

Data Engineer Intern - Corning Incorporated




In the summer of 2022, I interned with Corning Incorporated in their Manufacturing, Technology and Engineering department as a data engineer. During my time in New York, I focused on integrating digital technologies such as Internet of Things (IoT) and automation, into production processes, a concept referred to as Industry 4.0. Specifically, I implemented a Messaging Query Telemetry Transport (MQTT) broker as an intermediary to facilitate communication between a temperature probe on a Programmable Logic Controller (PLC) and a data hub called HighByte. Using HighByte I was able to extract data from disparate sources and transform that data for analysis purposes. Additionally, I developed a Python interface to streamline data entry process into the MQTT broker.




Fleet Attendant - Starship


Starship is a technology company that uses autonomous delivery robots to provide food for people and businesses. In my role as a Fleet Attendant, I ran a seamless operation of a fleet making up 63 robots by maintaining their health and sanitation. My daily activities involved helping robots in unexpected situations, troubleshooting technical problems, and reporting them to the onsite team. Additionally, I performed repairs, conducted inventories, and implemented upgrades on the robot infrastructure.
